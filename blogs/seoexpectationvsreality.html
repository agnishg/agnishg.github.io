SEO .. Expectations vs Reality

Search Engine Optimisation (SEO) has had a huge impact on businesses and our personal lives over the years. It has been around for almost 3 decades, the concept gained limelight when Sergey Brin and Larry Page began building what would become the biggest, most recognised search engine to date: BackRub. Confused? Well, you’re right because BackRub became Google in 1997 and the domain registered in 1997. The algorithm slowly evolved over the years to reward high quality, original and relevant content. Here's a great infographic to know more on the its evolution - 25+ Years of SEO by Ryan Shelley.

However, the term has also had its fair share of dissonances in expectation vs reality.

The most common SEO misconception is the target to rank first. While most companies and executives know SEO is important, often the path to success and hence the goals are not clear. Some important things to consider when making this decision are:

  • What keywords do you want to rank for?
  • How many keywords to rank for?
  • How quickly do you want to rank?
  • How does your overall marketing strategy and goals align?

Here's 5 most common assumptions which might prove costly in the long run.

1. Expectation : I can Rank immediately

Reality : Organic SEO can take up to 6 months

SEO is a strategic, which means you get dividends over longer periods of time, in most cases from one to six months in the minimum and compounding over time.

Optimally SEO goals should be set with a 1 to 3 year vision. Although the results might be slow, SEO brings huge dividends only when the work is constant, with high quality and relevant content to build your domain authority on the subjects. The content strategy needs to be perfectly aligned to gain maximum leverage.

2. Expectation : Revenues will go up proportionally

Reality : Nurturing capabilities will decide conversions

Good SEO strategies increase organic traffic to your site, this doesn’t immediately result in proportionally increased sales. This would depend on factors such as user search intent, keywords targeted and most importantly user experience on your website. Furthermore, once your strategy has had time to build up organic traffic, you must take advantage of it and give them the direction and nurturing they need to convert. Good lead nurturing capabilities and integrating SEO correctly in the buyer's journey is required to truly reap the benefits of SEO.

3. Expectation: Use As Many Keywords as Possible

Reality: Topics > Keywords

In the early days of Google, the ranking system was pretty rudimentary. The more a certain phrase (or keyword) appeared on a page, the more likely it was to come up in search results.

Since then, SEO algorithms have become more and more sophisticated. Keyword stuffing no longer help rank – rather might lead to penalisation. Focusing on topic clusters and understanding user search intent is a much better strategy. For example, instead of using “laptop” as a keyword, one might use something more specific topics such as “laptop for business professionals” or long term keywords such as “What is the best laptop model for college student”. Creating high quality content on variations of these topics and long tail keywords will help build authority on multiple small topics, contributing to credibility on the overall cluster.

4. Expectation : A Website Only Need On-Page SEO To Drive Traffic

Reality : The Off-page SEO Is Also Important In Driving Traffic To Your Website

It is widely believed that performing on-page activities is sufficient for generating traffic.In search engine optimisation strategies, domain authority is one of the key parameters Google uses to determine ranking. Having high quality backlinks generated from experts and high ranking pages help not only attract traffic, but also significantly contribute to your authority on the topic. Whenever the content reaches new platforms and audiences, page SEO score increases. You will be able to get more credibility, and effectively higher rankings in the search engines through this.

5. Expectation : High Search Volume Keywords are Goldmine

Reality: Target long tail keywords with better search intent clarity.

There are some keywords that will be difficult rank first for. Having clarity on this from the start is crucial. Investing time and resources for generic keywords that are dominated by big names with deep pockets is a pitfall one should avoid. Instead, long-tail keywords, including location-based queries, are more likely to pay off in the long run.
